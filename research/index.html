Featured Research

Reducing Visual Discomfort with HMDs
Using Dynamic Depth of Field

Although head-mounted displays (HMDs) are ideal devices for personal viewing of immersive stereoscopic content, exposure to VR applications on HMDs can result in adverse physical reactions (eye fatigue, headaches, nausea, and sweating). This study shows that depth-of-field blur can decrease (approximately 30%) overall visual discomfort with HMDs.

Sky Browser: Search for HDR Sky Maps

In a visual effects studio for movie production, sky maps play an important role for acting as a sky backdrop to a scene. We define a feature space that supports an interactive search function for HDR sky maps, allowing users to find ideal images based on the scene's appearance.

Perceptually Optimised Illumination for Seamless Composites

Realistic illumination in composition is important for a seamless mixture between the virtual and real world objects in visual effects and mixed reality. Here we find perceptually optimised thresholds for reducing resources across resolution and dynamic range of the radiance map for image based lighting.

A Pose Space for Squash and Stretch Deformation

'Squash & Stretch' is a fundamental technique in traditional animation, in which the shape of a character or object is intentionally distorted in order to accentuate its movement. We contribute a novel technique in which the squash & stretch effect can be introduced and controlled simply by specifying examples of the desired effect at any point along an animation timeline.
